# IRL-013 — Executive Research Memorandum

**Current State of the Invariant Research Programme**
**Status:** Partner Brief

## Purpose

This memorandum accompanies the current findings report and provides a concise
overview of where the programme stands today.

## Current Position

The Invariant Research Lab is no longer attempting to demonstrate *whether*
invariants exist. The evidence increasingly suggests they do. The research focus
has now shifted toward understanding:

- how they are discovered,
- how they are validated,
- how they are composed,
- how they are projected, and
- how they reduce reasoning effort.

## Current Evidence

The programme now contains four complementary research streams.

**Foundational Validation.** Demonstrates that curated invariant collections can
preserve semantic fidelity across multiple representations while exhibiting
constitutional restraint and reducing reasoning cost (Reasoning Economics).

**Platform Sovereignty.** Shows that constitutional operation can survive
provider substitution without depending on a single commercial AI provider.

**Invariant Intelligence.** Investigates the mechanism by which intent projects
onto governing invariant sets. Current work has shifted from lexical comparison
toward abstraction-aware causal fidelity.

**Instrument Validation.** Demonstrates that the underlying projection engines
are deterministic, stable, and reproducible *before* being relied upon for
scientific observation.

## Emerging Research — Invariant Discovery

Rather than assuming invariant collections already exist, the platform now
investigates how they may be discovered directly from large bodies of evidence.
Financial Services serves as the initial domain because of its unusually rich
regulatory corpus.

## Current Scientific Direction

Research is now progressing through a recursive hierarchy — each stage
independently *earned* through recurrence rather than assumed:

```
Evidence
  ↓
Domain Invariants
  ↓
Sub-domain Invariants
  ↓
Cross-domain Comparison
  ↓
Constitutional Invariants
  ↓
Universal Invariants (hypothesis)
```

## Near-term Objectives

- Complete the Financial Services baseline
- Validate Discovery Engine Phase 2
- Complete Compare
- Build the invariant topology graph
- Validate parent-child inheritance
- Begin a second industry
- Cross-domain comparison

## Closing

The programme remains exploratory. Many encouraging results have emerged. None
are considered universal until independently replicated across multiple domains.
