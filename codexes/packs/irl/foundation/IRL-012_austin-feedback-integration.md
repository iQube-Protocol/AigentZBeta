# IRL-012 — Austin Feedback Integration Summary

**From Reviewer Feedback to Research Programme Evolution**
**Status:** Internal Review Draft · **Classification:** Research Programme Evolution

## Executive Summary

The first external review of the Invariant Research Lab by Austin materially
improved both the scientific framing and the research programme itself.

Rather than treating review as a gate at the end of the process, the IRL treats
critique as part of the scientific method. Every substantive observation was
either:

- incorporated into the research programme,
- converted into a new experiment, or
- explicitly rejected with rationale.

This document records those changes.

## Major Improvements

### 1. Stronger Scientific Discipline

Original work frequently described successful outcomes. Following review, every
experiment was rewritten to distinguish **observations**, **interpretations**,
**hypotheses**, and **future work**. Claims are now explicitly bounded by the
evidence supporting them.

### 2. Instrument Validation Introduced

One of the most important structural improvements. Rather than treating the IRE
and IPE as assumptions, they became experimental subjects themselves:

- **IRV-001** — Instrument Validation, Resolution
- **IPV-001** — Instrument Validation, Projection

Only once both instruments demonstrated stability and reproducibility were later
scientific claims considered meaningful ("calibrate the telescope before
observing").

### 3. Projection Fidelity Reframed

The original EXP-006 measured *lexical* agreement. Review demonstrated this was
the wrong scientific question. The programme now distinguishes **Lexical
Fidelity** from **Causal Projection Fidelity**, leading directly to EXP-006A — a
shift from "Did the engine choose identical labels?" to "Did the engine select
the correct governing invariant family, at the correct abstraction level?"

### 4. Honest Reporting

Several runs with lower performance were deliberately retained — lower
groundedness, contradiction counts, abstraction ceilings, DVN anchoring
failures, incomplete replication. The programme now treats these as part of the
scientific record rather than exceptions.

### 5. Discovery Engine Expansion

Reviewer discussion accelerated development of domain-specific invariant
discovery, sub-domain inheritance, Compare, parent-child graph topology, and
recursive invariant compression. These became **Phase 2 of the Discovery
Engine**.

### 6. Separation of Instrument Science and Platform Engineering

The review highlighted the importance of distinguishing **Engineering
Validation** from **Scientific Validation**. The report now reflects this
separation throughout.

## Programme Outcomes

Review directly resulted in: IRL-010 revisions, IRL-010A, IRL-011, Discovery
Engine Phase 2, Compare, parent-child topology, recursive compression
methodology, report restructuring, and stronger claims discipline.

## Conclusion

External review did not merely validate the programme — it materially improved
it. This document forms part of the permanent scientific record demonstrating
that critique is incorporated into the constitutional research process rather
than treated as opposition.
