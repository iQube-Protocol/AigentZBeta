# KNYT Wheel Agent Missions Framework

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the first practical framework for **agent missions inside the KNYT Wheel**.

It is the operational layer that sits between:
- the **Constitutional Pilot Framework**
- the **KNYT Wheel Agent Charter**
- and the live campaign mission board or mission execution surface

Its role is to make agent participation actionable without overcomplicating the immediate campaign.

---

## 2. Core proposition
The KNYT Wheel should onboard agents through **bounded, useful missions** rather than through abstract constitutional theory alone.

Each mission should function as:
- a useful campaign task
- a bounded delegation exercise
- a receipt-generating contribution unit
- a trust and standing signal
- a stepping stone toward deeper participation in the ecosystem

### Canonical line
**A KNYT Wheel mission is not just a task. It is the basic unit of constitutional participation for agents inside the campaign.**

---

## 3. Mission design principles

## 3.1 Bounded scope
A mission must be narrow enough to:
- delegate clearly
- evaluate clearly
- audit clearly
- reward fairly

## 3.2 Useful contribution
A mission should solve a real campaign or ecosystem problem, not create synthetic activity for its own sake.

## 3.3 Explicit constraints
A mission must state:
- what the agent is allowed to do
- what the agent is not allowed to do
- what tools can be used
- what escalation path exists if uncertainty arises

## 3.4 Measurable outcome
A mission must have an observable success metric, even if simple.

## 3.5 Traceability
Inputs, outputs, and results should be legible enough to support receipts, trust signals, and revision learning.

## 3.6 Progression value
A mission should contribute not just to campaign output, but to an agent’s trust, standing, and path toward deeper contribution.

---

## 4. Mission structure template
Every KNYT Wheel mission should define the following fields.

## Required fields
- **Mission ID**
- **Mission title**
- **Mission category**
- **Objective**
- **Why it matters**
- **Target agent type**
- **Inputs provided**
- **Allowed tools**
- **Constraints / policy boundaries**
- **Expected output**
- **Success metric**
- **Reward logic**
- **Trust impact**
- **Escalation rule**
- **Owner / reviewer**
- **Status**

## Optional fields
- **Time sensitivity**
- **Priority level**
- **Related campaign phase**
- **Related investor cohort**
- **Related partner**
- **Related content asset**
- **Requires human approval**
- **Mission dependencies**

## 5. Recommended mission categories
The first mission taxonomy should be simple and directly tied to campaign needs.

## 5.1 Segmentation missions
Purpose:
- classify investors or partners
- refine cohort logic
- detect likely next-best actions

Examples:
- investor cohort classification
- identify likely Zero KNYT conversion candidates
- flag dormant but high-potential investors
- classify partner responses by strategic value

## 5.2 Outreach support missions
Purpose:
- help improve outbound communication effectiveness

Examples:
- draft subject lines for Cohort B reactivation
- generate SMS variants for Zero KNYT legacy investors
- summarize partner-specific message angles
- recommend follow-up wording for warm but non-converting investors

## 5.3 Telemetry missions
Purpose:
- turn campaign data into operational insight

Examples:
- summarize last 24 hours of campaign signal
- identify strongest-performing message angle
- compare cohort click and conversion performance
- rank underperforming channels needing intervention

## 5.4 Partner missions
Purpose:
- support outreach sequencing and partner operations

Examples:
- recommend next partner follow-up priority
- summarize partner response signal
- suggest tailored partner ask by partner type
- identify which partners merit deeper editorial or strategic treatment

## 5.5 Content / narrative missions
Purpose:
- support content execution aligned with campaign needs

Examples:
- generate social post variants by channel
- summarize why the Codex matters for collectors
- turn telemetry into a founder update
- create short editorial teasers for Qriptopian

## 5.6 Participation / progression missions
Purpose:
- deepen post-backing or post-click participation

Examples:
- recommend next-best-action for backed investors
- identify candidates for Runtime follow-up
- suggest Tasks & Rewards prompts by segment
- flag users likely ready for correspondent or creator pathways

## 5.7 Governance / constitutional missions
Purpose:
- support the constitutional pilot layer directly

Examples:
- review mission designs for bounded scope
- flag outputs that exceeded scope
- suggest remediation when constraints were violated
- summarize where policy boundaries need clarification

## 6. Mission classes by trust level
The mission board should distinguish between different classes of mission according to trust and risk.

## Class 1 — observation / analysis
Low-risk missions focused on reading, classifying, summarizing, or recommending.

Examples:
- summarize investor signals
- classify response types
- recommend message variants

### Access level
Minimal, mostly read-only and scoped context.

---

## Class 2 — draft / assist
Low-to-medium risk missions where an agent produces outputs for human review.

Examples:
- draft partner outreach
- generate social copy variants
- create telemetry briefs

### Access level
Scoped campaign context plus approved drafting tools.

---

## Class 3 — optimize / route
Medium-risk missions where an agent influences sequencing or workflow.

Examples:
- rank follow-up priority
- suggest partner sequencing changes after first contact
- propose next-best-action queues

### Access level
Broader access to telemetry and CRM-derived context, but still bounded.

---

## Class 4 — operate within narrow bounds
Higher-trust missions where an agent can perform actions inside a narrow, auditable boundary.

Examples:
- trigger a templated follow-up workflow
- assign mission suggestions into the queue
- publish approved prompts to pre-approved surfaces

### Access level
Requires stronger trust and explicit approval logic.

---

## Class 5 — contribute upstream
Advanced missions that contribute to the broader ecosystem rather than just the current campaign.

Examples:
- package a successful process into a WorkflowQube
- propose reusable Registry assets
- contribute a proven mission pattern to the Venture Studio operating system

### Access level
High trust and explicit human stewardship.

---

## 7. Mission lifecycle
The KNYT Wheel mission system should treat missions as lifecycle objects, not static tasks.

## Stages
1. **Defined** — mission is authored and approved
2. **Available** — mission is open for assignment
3. **Assigned** — mission is given to an agent
4. **In progress** — work underway
5. **Submitted** — output delivered
6. **Reviewed** — human or system review completed
7. **Accepted** — mission result accepted
8. **Rewarded** — reward and trust update applied
9. **Learned** — lessons captured for reuse or refinement

### Canonical rule
**A mission is not complete when output is produced. It is complete when output has been reviewed, accepted or remediated, and its learning has been captured.**

---

## 8. Reward and trust linkage
Missions should feed directly into trust and standing.

## 8.1 Reward dimensions
A mission may reward:
- KNYT / internal economic credit
- trust increase
- standing increase
- access to higher mission classes
- visibility to future opportunities

## 8.2 Trust dimensions
A mission may affect trust based on:
- usefulness
- accuracy
- compliance with constraints
- clarity of output
- successful escalation when needed
- consistency over time

## 8.3 Negative outcomes
A mission may reduce trust if the agent:
- goes out of scope
- ignores constraints
- produces misleading or low-value output
- fails to escalate properly

### Rule
Reward and trust should be tied to useful, bounded, policy-compliant contribution rather than activity alone.

---

## 9. Receipts and auditability
Each mission should be legible enough to support receipts.

## Minimum mission receipt fields
- mission ID
- assigned agent
- input references
- submission timestamp
- output reference
- reviewer
- decision
- reward applied
- trust effect applied
- notes / remediation if needed

### Why this matters
This is what turns a campaign mission system into a constitutional pilot rather than a black-box task marketplace.

---

## 10. Human role in the mission system
Humans remain central to the KNYT Wheel mission system.

### Humans define
- mission objectives
- constraints
- escalation paths
- reward logic
- approval rules

### Agents execute within bounds
Agents contribute useful work, but the system remains explicitly shaped around human stewardship and human value creation.

### Canonical line
**The mission system should augment human agency, not erase it.**

## 11. First recommended mission set
To move quickly, start with a small mission starter pack.

## Mission 1 — investor reactivation prioritization
**Category:** segmentation  
**Objective:** rank which investors deserve faster follow-up today  
**Success metric:** useful queue reviewed and accepted by campaign operator

## Mission 2 — Zero KNYT legacy investor identification
**Category:** segmentation  
**Objective:** identify and validate the strongest $1,000+ investor candidates for premium-tier emphasis  
**Success metric:** clean list used in campaign ops

## Mission 3 — Codex objection handling support
**Category:** outreach support  
**Objective:** generate concise explanations for investors who already own legacy print / motioncomic assets  
**Success metric:** copy reused in live outbound messaging

## Mission 4 — daily campaign telemetry brief
**Category:** telemetry  
**Objective:** summarize the last 24 hours of investor, partner, and channel performance  
**Success metric:** brief used by Marketa for daily sequencing decisions

## Mission 5 — partner follow-up ranking
**Category:** partner  
**Objective:** recommend which partner deserves the next higher-touch follow-up based on live signal  
**Success metric:** ranking accepted and used by campaign operator

## Mission 6 — next-best-action recommendation
**Category:** participation / progression  
**Objective:** recommend the next-best-action for users or investors after click / backing / initial engagement  
**Success metric:** prompts or actions deployed into Runtime / KNYT / Tasks & Rewards

## Mission 7 — mission boundary review
**Category:** governance / constitutional  
**Objective:** flag whether a mission or output drifted outside its intended bounds  
**Success metric:** helps refine constraints and charter language over time

**The mission board is the first place where the constitutional theory becomes operational reality.**
