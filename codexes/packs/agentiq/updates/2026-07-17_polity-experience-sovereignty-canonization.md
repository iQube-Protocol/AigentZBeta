## One line per newly seeded invariant

- **207 The experience layer is the control layer** — in a cybernetic society experience is governance; the experience layer is a control surface, not a cosmetic one.
- **208 Access is not sovereignty** — the platform bargain made users active inside systems they did not govern; the cost was experience extraction, not only data extraction.
- **209 The sovereign runtime as the new civic unit** — the account made the user legible to the platform; the runtime makes the world accountable to the user.
- **210 The experience boundary** — a runtime's boundary is a living policy surface governing what may enter attention, action, and influence; sacred means governed, not sealed.
- **211 The three filtering tiers** — Registry for builders, Studio for composers, Runtime for everyone (Catalogue = approved activation); software outputs are inputs, not endpoints.
- **212 The time dividend** — the first measurable dividend of experience sovereignty is time returned under the user's authority; return minutes to meaning, not to more tasks.
- **213 Convenience is not the enemy — false convenience is** — false convenience defers repair into the future; privacy creates trust and trust = speed = convenience.
- **214 Experience vibing** — the next abstraction after software: composing governed agentic experiences without becoming a technologist; vibe-coding's best outputs are its inputs.
- **215 The people closest to consequence** — should shape the experience without writing code; anti-bottleneck, not anti-technical, and developers matter more as trusted substrate.
- **216 Sovereignty is a ladder, not a switch** — sovereignty is developed through participation; PCS is polity formation, since a polity cannot be built from passive users.
- **217 The Progressive Creative Sovereignty ladder** — Consumer → Citizen → Curator → Remixer → Creator → Operator → Composer → Contributor → Steward; no one trapped as a consumer.
- **218 No one is sovereign in every domain** — sovereignty is portable but expertise is domain-specific; the polity develops people context by context.
- **219 A federation of development paths** — the polity is not one ladder but many paths (creative, operator, correspondent, technical, stewardship) toward greater agency.
- **220 Progressive sovereignty requires progressive accountability** — rising agency raises responsibility; the user can climb because the system can remember, attribute, verify, and repair.
- **221 Stewardship is sovereignty matured into responsibility** — stewardship sits beyond ownership, carrying care for continuity, canon, health, and the living system's long-term development.
- **222 The Experience Polity as sovereign cybernetic complex** — a living order of humans, agents, primitives, runtimes, experiences, receipts, and stewardship that increases agency rather than capturing it.
- **223 The agentic internet must increase human agency** — not merely an internet with AI agents, but one that makes humans more sovereign in a world of agents.
- **224 A feed captures attention; an agent can capture action** — the platform-agent internet can capture delegation; the problem is unaccountable agents in unaccountable systems.
- **225 Platforms became governments of experience** — governing belonging without accountability; the user performs citizenship work without citizenship rights (managed participation = rented agency).
- **226 The platform economy prices attention; the Experience Polity prices accountable contribution** — the platform asks what holds attention, the polity asks what deserves trust.
- **227 The polity must remain larger than the stack** — the metaMe / AgentiQ stack is one interpretation that must stay accountable to the broader polity; leadership is earned, not owned.
- **228 The stack is civic architecture** — each layer has a civic function; Studio composes, Catalogue approves, Runtime makes livable, receipts make accountable, repair keeps honest.
- **229 Bound agents, not unbound agents** — user sovereign, aigentMe as regent, helper-grade agents (myGuard/myClaw/myAnima/myBot) as bounded staff, avoiding ungoverned agent swarms.
- **230 Portable belonging, not audience captivity** — belonging must not depend on an account a platform can revoke; the user should not rent access to relationships they built.
- **231 Bounded delegation increases civic bandwidth** — bounded delegation makes personal sovereignty operational at scale, letting citizens participate with more context and less exhaustion.
- **232 Proof of Sovereignty Expansion (PoSE)** — the legitimacy test: a system is legitimate only if users become more sovereign as it becomes more capable.
- **233 The dimensions of PoSE** — Time, Agency, Portability, Delegation Control, Creative Progression, Accountability, Context, Contribution Recognition, Anti-Capture, Stewardship Health; leadership earned by measurable expansion.
- **234 Sovereignty is consciousness capable of action** — sovereignty is a pathway of consciousness requiring conscious agency; metaMe does not merely serve the user, it develops the person.
