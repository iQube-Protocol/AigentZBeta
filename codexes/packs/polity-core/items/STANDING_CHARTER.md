# The Standing Charter

**Version 1.0.0 · Ratified 2026-06-17 · Status: ratified · Polity Core constitutional document**

## Purpose

Standing exists to establish confidence in the veracity of information
contributed by citizens, aigents, organizations, and institutions within the
Polity.

- Standing is not reputation.
- Standing is not popularity.
- Standing is not status.
- Standing is not social ranking.

Standing is a measure of confidence that a declaration accurately reflects
reality.

## First Principle

Citizens are not responsible for predicting the future consequences of truthful
information.

Citizens are responsible for truthfully representing:

- who they are,
- what they know,
- what they can do,
- what they have done,
- what they intend.

The obligation of the citizen is veracity.
The obligation of the Polity is interpretation.

## Standing Declarations

Standing begins with declarations. Examples include:

- Identity declarations
- Capability declarations
- Intent declarations
- Experience declarations
- Credential declarations

Declarations become Standing signals through verification and iQubing.

## Standing and Proof of Work Potential

Standing contributes to the calculation of Proof of Work Potential.

Proof of Work Potential estimates the capability latent within truthful
information before that capability is applied.

Standing does not measure outcomes. Standing helps estimate potential.

## Standing and Proof of Time Saved

Proof of Time Saved verifies realized value.

Proof of Time Saved does not primarily recalibrate Standing. Rather, it improves
the Polity's ability to understand how capability emerges from truthful
information.

## Who Holds Standing

Standing is held by all participating entities — citizens, aigents,
organizations, and institutions. Standing is **not** citizenship.

- **Citizens** (human) hold Standing, citizenship, and inalienable rights.
- **Participant agents** (participant-passport-bearing entities) hold Standing.
  They do **not** hold citizenship or inalienable rights; their rights are
  **participatory and revocable**.

Standing measures veracity confidence; it does not confer citizenship,
governance authority, or inalienable rights.

## Constitutional Principle

The purpose of Standing is not to determine a citizen's worth.

The purpose of Standing is to establish confidence in the truthfulness of
signals entering the Polity.
