Abstract
The dollar signals price. Bitcoin signals state. COYN signals consequential knowledge work.
The first three papers in this series established a progression: information is not free because it is consequential;
consequence is lived in time; and time saved must be proven through accountable state change. This paper asks
how that proof becomes economically legible.
Money has historically been one of humanity’s most powerful information classes because it collapses value into
price. The U.S. dollar became the dominant global signal currency for price, liquidity, institutional trust, and
settlement. Bitcoin introduced a second major signal class: a decentralized monetary system that binds work,
time, and state into a tamper-resistant ledger. COYN introduces a third class of signal currency: a currency for
verified knowledge work, information risk, time-value, and accountable consequence.
COYN — Currency Of Your Net-work — is not money for data. It is a signal currency for the sovereign
knowledge economy. It measures the relationship between information, work potential, risk, time saved, repair
avoided, and verified state change. Where money collapses value into price, and information collapses value into
time, COYN re-expands price into context.
This paper argues that COYN becomes credible as a signal currency when information risk, work potential, and
time-value can be predicted, verified, underwritten, transacted, and accounted for. Proof of Work Potential
predicts what information may do. Proof of Time Saved verifies what it did. Insurance and actuarial
underwriting can price the possibility of time-to-repair. Performance-based settlement can align payment with
verified time-to-value. iQubes can preserve rights, provenance, and derivative value. Qc can settle micro-events.
DVN receipts can record consequence. COYN can capture the higher-order risk-value-time signal.

1. From Proof to Currency
PoWP + PoTS = Provable Time Sovereignty.
Proof of Work Potential predicts what information may do. Proof of Time Saved verifies what it did. Together,
they allow a human, agent, institution, market, or polity to assess whether information actually compressed
time-to-value-adding-state-change or expanded time-to-repair.
But once work potential and time saved can be predicted and verified, a new question emerges: how should that
verified knowledge work become economically legible?
This is the purpose of COYN. COYN does not begin from the premise that all information should be bought and
sold. It begins from the premise that consequential knowledge work should be legible, accountable, and
rewardable. Some information should remain open. Some should be protected. Some should be freely shared.
Some should be restricted. Some should be compensated. Some should never be activated for certain intents at
any price.
COYN is not a universal paywall for information. It is a signal currency for verified informational consequence.
2. Money as an Information Class
Money is often treated as if it were separate from information, but money is itself a form of information.
A unit of money is powerful not because of its physical form, but because of what it signals. A banknote, coin,
ledger entry, or token is an information object that represents claim, trust, exchangeability, settlement, and
capacity to command work.
Money is therefore one of the most transmutable information classes humans have created. It can be converted
across many contexts: food, labor, land, shelter, transport, compute, media, energy, services, debt, care, security,
and optionality.
Money collapses value into price.
That collapse makes markets possible. But it also distorts what cannot be fully expressed by price. Price may
reflect demand, scarcity, liquidity, trust, speculation, bargaining power, convenience, coercion, or manipulation.
It does not necessarily reflect dignity, ecological consequence, risk exposure, creative origin, repair burden, time
saved, time stolen, or public value.
Price is one signal about value. It is not the whole truth of value.
3. The Dollar as Price Signal Currency
The U.S. dollar is the dominant signal currency of global price.

It signals liquidity, institutional trust, settlement capacity, reserve value, and market comparability. It allows
assets, commodities, labor, services, debt, and risk to be priced across global markets.
What is this worth in market price?
That is a powerful question, but it is not sufficient for the agentic knowledge economy. Information does not
behave like a simple commodity. Its value changes by context, intent, actor, risk, timing, provenance,
permission, and activation pathway.
The dollar can price access, services, labor, and assets. But it does not natively express the full context of
information activation. It can price a dataset. It cannot, by itself, determine whether activating that dataset will
save time, create risk, violate rights, reduce repair, or produce valid knowledge work.
4. Bitcoin as State-Time Signal Currency
Bitcoin introduced a second major signal currency class. Bitcoin is often understood as digital money: scarce,
decentralized, censorship-resistant, and secured by proof of work. But its deeper signal function is that it binds
work, time, and state change into a tamper-resistant ledger.
What state was proven, in what order, under a work-secured ledger?
Bitcoin is not a truth machine. It does not prove that an external claim is true simply because a hash, transaction,
or commitment appears on-chain. But it does provide a durable timechain for anchoring proof commitments.
This matters because state history is foundational to trust. If a system cannot reliably answer what changed,
when, and in what order, then dispute, manipulation, reconciliation burden, and institutional dependency expand.
PoW binds work to time for ledger security.
This gives Bitcoin a distinctive signal role. The dollar signals price. Bitcoin signals decentralized, time-ordered
state. COYN extends the signal landscape into another domain: verified knowledge work.

5. Information as Time-Value
Money collapses value into price. Information collapses value into time.
A map saves wandering. A diagnosis saves suffering. A proof saves uncertainty. A credential saves verification.
A clean dataset saves repair. A reliable iQube saves agentic compute. A trusted agent saves coordination. A bad
signal can waste years.
Information is valuable not only because someone will pay for it. It is valuable because it changes the
relationship between work and time. Good information compresses time-to-value. Bad information expands
time-to-repair.
The agentic economy does not merely need a currency that prices access to information. It needs a currency that
can signal the value of information as work potential, time compression, repair avoidance, risk reduction, and
accountable state change.
For humans, information can buy back time. For agents, information can make compute useful. For the polity,
information can reduce systemic time-to-repair.

7. The PoWP–PoTS Loop
PoWP is the promise. PoTS is the proof. COYN is the signal.
COYN measures the loop between Proof of Work Potential and Proof of Time Saved. Proof of Work Potential
asks what this information can do. Proof of Time Saved asks what it actually did to time-to-value or
time-to-repair. COYN asks how that prediction-verification loop should become an economic signal.
An iQube carries information, context, provenance, permissions, and risk. PoWP estimates the work potential of
that iQube under a defined context and intent. A human or agent activates the iQube. Work is performed. PoTS
verifies whether the work compressed time-to-value-adding-state-change. DVN records the consequence. Qc
settles micro-events. COYN updates the higher-order signal of value.
Prediction becomes activation. Activation becomes state change. State change becomes receipt.
Receipt becomes signal. Signal updates future prediction.

The PoWP–PoTS loop becomes economically powerful when it is built into the transaction model.
Most information markets price at access: pay, download; subscribe, read; license, use; unlock, consume. But if
information is work potential, then its real value is not fully known at access. Its value is realized after activation,
when the information produces or fails to produce a useful state change.
The value of an information asset should not be settled only at access. It should be settled after
activation.

A more complete transaction model includes a PoWP estimate, initial payment or escrow, activation, PoTS

9. Feedback as a Transaction Obligation
Access creates the right to use; activation creates the obligation to account.
Performance-based settlement requires feedback. If buyers can activate information assets, capture value, and
refuse to report outcomes, then the system cannot learn. If sellers can overstate work potential without
consequence, the system becomes speculative. If agents can claim time saved without receipts, the system
becomes gameable.
This does not mean exposing private or proprietary data. Feedback can be scoped, minimized, anonymized,
aggregated, or cryptographically proven where appropriate.

10. Controlled Use, Provenance, and Derivative Data Rights
Information value does not end at access. It compounds through use, reuse, transformation, and
derivation.
Performance-based settlement creates a more accurate information market, but it also introduces a new
challenge. If an information asset proves valuable — if it compresses time-to-value, reduces risk, or generates
high-value outputs — the buyer may be tempted to preserve, reuse, copy, recombine, or train on the asset beyond
the original transaction.
This is not always illegitimate. Information often becomes more valuable when it is cleaned, combined,
structured, validated, or transformed. A buyer may create a genuinely new derivative dataset with higher
performance than the original. That can itself be valuable knowledge work. But the economic model must
distinguish authorized transformation from unaccounted extraction.
The transaction model should define more than access rights. It should define activation rights, retention rights,
reuse rights, training rights, copy rights, derivative rights, resale rights, royalty obligations, provenance
obligations, and audit obligations.

For high-value or high-sensitivity information, controlled execution may become a major economic primitive.
Trusted execution environments, secure enclaves, server-side processing, encrypted payloads, output filtering,

activation without unnecessary exposure.
Controlled activation turns sensitive information into usable work potential without forcing payload

11. Risk as the Bridge to Economic Legibility
Risk is the bridge between work potential, time-to-repair, underwriting, and COYN.
An information asset does not only carry value potential. It carries risk potential. It may save time, but it may
also expand time-to-repair. It may reduce uncertainty, but it may also create privacy exposure, reputational harm,
safety failure, market distortion, model contamination, or governance risk.
This is why risk is central to Proof of Work Potential. PoWP should not merely estimate upside. It must estimate
downside: what harm could follow from activation, what repair burden could be created, what privacy or safety
risk exists, what verification burden is required, what downstream state changes could be triggered, what failure
would cost, and what it would take to underwrite that risk.
Risk is the bridge between work potential and economic legibility.
12. Underwriting and Risk-Veracity Signals
The most important bridge between COYN and conventional financial systems may be underwriting.
Insurance is one of society’s most mature mechanisms for pricing risk. If an information asset or iQube
activation pathway can be underwritten, that underwriting creates a risk-veracity signal. The premium is not
merely a cost. It is information.
It reveals expected probability of failure, severity of potential harm, quality of controls, confidence in
provenance, confidence in policy enforcement, expected repair burden, and the cost of making activation
tolerable.
If risk can be underwritten, it can become economically legible.
Underwritten risk turns abstract information risk into a transactional cost.
COYN can use that cost as a high-integrity input into value signaling. The result is a richer economic model:
PoWP predicts risk and work potential. PoTS verifies time-value or time-repair outcomes. Underwriting prices
the possibility of failure. COYN captures the risk-value-time signal.
Insurance prices the possibility of time-to-repair. COYN prices the realized and predicted value of
time-to-value.

14. Qc and COYN
Qc settles the event. COYN captures the signal.
COYN should be distinguished from QriptoCENT, or Qc. Qc is the micro-settlement rail. It can settle the small,
frequent, deterministic events of agentic knowledge work: access, activation, inference, curation, validation,
review, contribution, and other micro-events.
COYN is the higher-order signal currency. It captures accumulated value from knowledge work, risk reduction,
time saved, repair avoided, and verified contribution. A single activation may be small. A pattern of verified
activations can reveal high work potential, low repair burden, strong time compression, and trustworthy
contribution.
Qc operates at the transactional layer. COYN operates at the signal layer. Both are necessary.

15. COYN and the Value of Human-Agent Networks
COYN is Currency Of Your Net-work. The spelling matters because value in the agentic economy is produced
through networks of work.
A human does not create alone. An agent does not act alone. An iQube does not generate value until activated. A
model does not become useful without context. A tool does not produce value without purpose. A market does
not discover value without signals. A polity does not govern consequence without receipts.
Value emerges from the net-work: the relationships among humans, agents, information assets, permissions,
tools, models, receipts, risk, and consequence. COYN makes this net-work visible.
COYN is the currency of consequential net-work.
16. Human Time, Agentic Compute, and COYN
Human time is existential. Agentic time is operational. Sovereign cybernetics must govern both.
For humans, COYN should recognize value when knowledge work restores time, reduces waiting, improves
decisions, prevents repair, protects attention, or increases agency. For agents, COYN should recognize value
when good information reduces test-time compute, retrieval burden, failed tool calls, retries, ambiguity,
verification loops, and repair cycles.
For humans, bad information wastes life. For agents, bad information burns cycles. COYN should signal the
difference.
This matters because the agentic economy will be full of work that is invisible to ordinary price systems: context
curation, permissioning, validation, risk scoring, repair avoidance, tool orchestration, model selection, agent
supervision, trust maintenance, provenance checking, and time-saving coordination. These forms of knowledge
work are economically real even when they are hard to price through conventional markets.

17. Market Demand and Vickrey Discovery
Market demand still matters. COYN does not reject markets. It rejects the idea that price alone exhausts value.
For certain information activations, qualified market demand can reveal expected value. A Vickrey-style
mechanism, where bidders reveal expected value under defined conditions, can help discover what an activation
is worth to qualified participants.
But demand must sit inside governance. Risk comes first. Policy comes first. Permission comes first. Rights
come first. Market demand can discover price. It cannot legitimize impermissible consequence.
Market demand can discover price. It cannot purchase permission to externalize harm.
18. COYN and the Redefinition of the Knowledge Economy
The knowledge economy has often been defined by expertise, intellectual property, information goods, services,
and intangible value. But the agentic age requires a deeper definition.
The sovereign knowledge economy is not merely an economy where knowledge is produced and sold. It is an
economy where knowledge work can be verified, where information risk can be governed, where time saved can
be proven, where derivative value can be tracked, and where consequence can be accounted for.
The old knowledge economy rewarded access, credentials, ownership, and output. The sovereign knowledge
economy rewards work potential, valid activation, risk reduction, time saved, repair avoided, compute
efficiency, trusted context, controlled use, derivative contribution, accountable consequence, and contribution to
the polity.
The Knowledge Economy becomes sovereign when work potential and time saved can both be
proven.
19. The Third Signal Currency
The dollar signals price. Bitcoin signals state. COYN signals consequential knowledge work.
The dollar is the dominant signal currency of price and liquidity. Bitcoin is the dominant signal currency of
decentralized, proof-of-work-secured monetary state. COYN is the emerging signal currency of
information-as-work-potential, verified time-value, risk, consequence, and sovereign knowledge work.
This is not a claim that COYN replaces the dollar or Bitcoin. It is a claim that COYN addresses a different signal
domain.
The dollar answers: what is this worth in price? Bitcoin answers: what state was proven in a decentralized
timechain? COYN answers: what work potential was activated, what time-value was realized, what risk was
governed, and what consequence was verified?

20. Conclusion: From Price to Time-Value

that consequence is lived in time. The third argued that time saved must be proven through a loop between Proof
of Work Potential and Proof of Time Saved.
This paper has argued that once work potential and time saved can be predicted and verified, they require a new
economic signal. That signal is COYN.
