Abstract
Proof of Work Potential predicts what information may do. Proof of Time Saved verifies what it did.
The previous paper in this series, Time Sovereignty, argued that time is the citizen’s primary currency and that
information sovereignty is inseparable from sovereignty over time. If information directs work, work consumes
time, and time is where consequence is lived, then any system that claims to save time must prove what it saved,
for whom, at what cost, and with what consequence.
This paper introduces Proof of Time Saved as a framework for verified acceleration toward valid outcomes. It is
not a productivity metric for rewarding speed alone. Speed without validity creates slop, safety failures,
misinformation, privacy risk, repair burden, and displaced cost. A fast bad output is not time saved. It is repair
deferred.
The paper also introduces Proof of Work Potential as the predictive counterpart to Proof of Time Saved. Proof of
Work Potential estimates what useful work an information asset, iQube, model, tool, workflow, or agent may be
capable of producing under a defined context, intent, risk profile, and activation path. Proof of Time Saved
verifies whether that predicted work potential actually compressed time-to-value-adding-state-change after
activation.
PoWP + PoTS = Provable Time Sovereignty.
The paper positions iQubes as evidence containers for this loop because they can carry the context, provenance,
permissions, risk, value, activation conditions, and receipts required to make time-saving claims auditable. It
also positions Bitcoin as a useful proof architecture reference: not merely as a monetary network, but as a
proof-of-work-secured timechain capable of anchoring commitments for iQubes, PoWP, PoTS, and consequence
receipts.

1. From Time Sovereignty to Proof
Information directs work. Work consumes time. Time is where consequence is lived.
If that is true, then time saving cannot be treated as a casual claim. It must be proven.
A platform may claim to save time by automating a task while quietly increasing verification burden elsewhere.
A model may produce an answer quickly while forcing a human to spend hours checking whether the answer is
true. An agent may complete a workflow quickly while creating privacy risk, repair burden, or unsafe
downstream action.
That is not time saved. It is time shifted.
Time saved is only valuable when it reduces the total time required to reach a valid, value-adding
outcome without displacing cost, risk, or repair burden elsewhere.
This is why Proof of Time Saved belongs after Time Sovereignty. Without time sovereignty, time saved can
become just another extractive productivity metric. With time sovereignty, Proof of Time Saved becomes a
mechanism of restoration.
Proof of Time Saved only matters because Time Sovereignty matters.
2. From Work Potential to Time Saved
The first paper in this series framed iQubes as containers of work potential. The second paper argued that work is
expressed through time. This paper now asks: how do we prove that work potential became time-value?
Proof of Work Potential, or PoWP, is predictive. It estimates what useful work an information asset, iQube,
model, tool, workflow, or agent may be capable of producing under a given context, intent, risk profile, and
activation pathway.
PoWP asks what work this information might enable, what value it could produce, what risk it could reduce,
what repair it could prevent, what time it could save, what agentic compute it could make more useful, and under
what conditions it should be activated.
Proof of Time Saved, or PoTS, is verificational. It verifies whether that predicted work potential actually
compressed time-to-value-adding-state-change after activation.
PoWP predicts what information may do. PoTS verifies what it did.
Work potential without verification becomes speculation. Verification without prediction cannot guide action.
The agentic economy needs both.

3. The False Promise of Speed
The old platform ethos was move fast and break things. That ethos treated speed as progress and damage as an
externality. It rewarded acceleration even when the cost of that acceleration was borne by users, workers,
communities, creators, regulators, moderators, institutions, ecosystems, or the public.
In information systems, this logic produces familiar harms: low-quality outputs, unsafe automation, slop,
misinformation, hallucinations, privacy violations, broken trust, repair burden, rushed decisions, hidden
moderation costs, displaced labor, and institutional fatigue.
Speed is not value. Valid acceleration is value.
A system has not saved time merely because it produced something quickly. It has saved time only if it helped
reach a better state sooner. A fast bad answer is not time saved. A fast unsafe action is not time saved. A fast
hallucination is not time saved.
A fast bad output is not time saved. It is repair deferred.
Move fast and fix things.
4. Time-to-Output vs Time-to-Value-Adding-State-Change
A central mistake in automation is confusing time-to-output with time-to-value. Time-to-output is the time
required to produce a visible artifact, answer, recommendation, transaction, file, message, image, report, action,
or decision. It is easy to measure because something appears. But appearance is not value.
Time-to-value is the time required to produce a useful and meaningful improvement. It asks whether the output
changed something for the better: did it reduce uncertainty, help a decision, complete a task, reduce risk,
improve coordination, prevent repair, or increase agency?
The measure is not time-to-output. It is time-to-value-adding-state-change.
A value-adding state change is not merely an output. It is an outcome that is useful, valid, accountable,
permissioned, context-aligned, risk-aware, and legitimately consequential.

5. Time-to-Repair: The Shadow Ledger of Bad Information
Good information compresses time-to-value. Bad information expands time-to-repair.
Proof of Time Saved must account not only for the time required to produce an outcome, but for the time
required to repair the consequences of bad information, bad action, or bad automation.
Time-to-repair includes the time required to detect error, verify claims, reverse bad decisions, correct records,
restore trust, recover from privacy violations, fix broken outputs, redo work, moderate harmful content, retrain
models, compensate harmed parties, repair relationships, resolve disputes, rebuild institutional confidence, and
recover from misdirected action.
Bad information does not merely waste time in the present. It imposes work on the future.
Time-to-repair is the shadow ledger of bad information.
A proof that ignores repair burden is not Proof of Time Saved. It is proof of displaced cost.
6. Bitcoin, Proof of Work, and the Ledger of Time
Bitcoin provides a powerful example of why proof systems matter. It is often evaluated through the energetic
cost of proof-of-work mining. If the Bitcoin network is understood only as a mechanism for minting and
transferring bitcoin, then the value of its output is assessed primarily through the market value and utility of BTC
as a cryptoasset.
But this is an incomplete view of the network’s work. Bitcoin can also be understood as a decentralized,
tamper-resistant information management system: a timechain that binds work, time, and state change into a
globally verifiable ledger. From this perspective, its output is not only bitcoin. Its output is also ordered state
history, anti-gaming infrastructure, settlement finality, censorship resistance, and a durable substrate for
anchoring proof commitments.
Bitcoin is not a truth machine. It is a timechain for anchoring proof commitments.
Bitcoin spends energy and compute to reduce the need for certain trusted intermediaries, reconciliation
processes, ledger disputes, state manipulation, censorship risks, and institutional repair burdens. It compresses
time-to-trust for decentralized settlement by making ledger manipulation costly and state history difficult to
rewrite.

7. The Cybernetic Accounting Horizon
Proof of Time Saved must be holistic. A system may look expensive or inefficient if judged only by immediate
output. But its net value may be far higher if it reduces systemic repair, trust failure, dispute, fraud, delay,
verification burden, or institutional overhead.
Conversely, a system may look efficient locally while being extractive globally.
Local efficiency can be global extraction. Local cost can be global trust infrastructure.
A narrow accounting horizon asks whether a task completed faster. A wider accounting horizon asks whether the
intervention reduced total system time-to-value, including risk, repair, verification, dispute, environmental cost,
and downstream consequence.
The purpose of Proof of Time Saved is not to optimize isolated tasks. It is to measure whether information and
agentic work actually reduce the total time required to reach valid outcomes across a relevant system boundary.

8. The Proof of Time Saved Measurement Stack
A Proof of Time Saved event should be composed from several layers: Proof of Work Potential, baseline time,
actual time, validity threshold, repair adjustment, risk adjustment, human time saved, agentic compute saved,
and consequence receipt.

PoTS = Baseline Time − Actual Time − Repair Burden − Risk Burden, after validity clears
No validity, no proof.
Baseline time is the estimated or observed time required to reach the same valid outcome without the
intervention. Actual time is the measured time required with the intervention. The validity threshold ensures that
no time is counted as saved unless the outcome is correct, useful, safe, permissioned, and aligned with the
governing context.
Repair adjustment subtracts downstream correction, review, remediation, trust repair, privacy remediation, or
human/agent time spent undoing consequences. Risk adjustment can reduce or invalidate claimed time saved
when activation increases privacy, safety, ecological, reputational, or governance risk.
PoTS = Baseline Time − Actual Time − Repair Burden − Risk Burden, after validity clears.

10. Risk, Repair, and Underwriting
Risk is the bridge between work potential, time-to-repair, insurance, and COYN pricing. At the heart of Proof of
Work Potential is risk assessment. An information asset does not merely carry useful potential. It also carries a
risk footprint.
If the risk associated with an information asset can be quantified with sufficient rigor, then in principle it can be
underwritten. Insurers and actuaries exist to price risk. Their premiums, exclusions, limits, and underwriting
requirements reveal the expected cost of failure, the severity of potential repair, the confidence in controls, and
the price of making the risk tolerable.

11. Provable Time Sovereignty
PoWP + PoTS = Provable Time Sovereignty.
Provable Time Sovereignty is the ability to predict and verify how information changes the relationship between
work and time.
For humans, it asks whether information restored attention, reduced waiting, reduced labor, reduced repair,
improved decision-making, and increased agency. For agents, it asks whether information reduced test-time
compute, retries, failed tool calls, ambiguity, verification burden, and converted compute into valid work. For
the polity, it asks whether the intervention reduced total system time-to-value and made consequence
accountable.
The Knowledge Economy becomes sovereign when work potential and time saved can both be
proven.
12. Quality, Safety, and Accountability Gates
Proof of Time Saved must not reward speed alone. It must pass gates: quality, safety, consent, rights, policy,
provenance, human oversight where required, ecological or social consequence, repair burden, and auditability.
These gates prevent the system from mistaking reckless acceleration for value. A medical agent should not claim
time saved if it bypasses safety review. A legal agent should not claim time saved if it misses a material risk. A
creative agent should not claim time saved if it violates rights. A research agent should not claim time saved if it
fabricates citations.
Time saved only counts when quality, safety, rights, and accountability are preserved or improved.
Time saved is not value if it merely moves cost, risk, or burden elsewhere.

13. Anti-Gaming: Why Proof Needs a Ledger
Any proof system that creates value will be gamed. Potential failure modes include inflated baselines, weak
baselines, low-quality outputs produced quickly, hidden repair work, unpaid human review, ignored downstream
harm, speed rewarded over judgment, over-automation of sensitive tasks, artificial friction created to claim
savings, and local speed measured while system-wide cost is ignored.
Proof systems exist because claims are gameable.
This is why PoTS needs a ledgered proof architecture. Bitcoin’s Proof of Work demonstrates that ledger
integrity requires anti-gaming economics: rewriting history should be costly, and valid state ordering should be
collectively verifiable. PoTS needs an analogous principle for knowledge work.
It should not prove that arbitrary compute was spent. It should prove that informational work potential produced
valid, accountable time compression.
A reliable time-saving economy requires a reliable consequence ledger.
Time saved locally can become time stolen systemically.
14. iQubes as Evidence Containers
An iQube is a container of work potential. Proof of Time Saved proves when that potential actually saved time.
An iQube can contain or reference the information required to support a time-saving claim: source information,
metadata, provenance, permissions, context, ownership, risk profile, value profile, expected work potential,
predicted time compression, baseline assumptions, activation policy, agentic actions, outputs, validation
evidence, repair adjustments, consequence receipts, and historical PoTS performance.
iQubes make Proof of Time Saved auditable by carrying the context required to prove what changed.
iQubes are containers of work potential. PoTS proves when that potential saved time.
15. DVN Receipts and Proof of State
Proof of Time Saved cannot rely on self-reporting. A system should not be able to claim time saved merely
because it says so. It must be able to show a state change, the time delta, the actor, the information activated, the
permissions involved, the outcome, and the validation conditions.
A Proof of Time Saved receipt may record the initiating actor, delegated agent, iQube activated, declared intent,
permissions, baseline state, end state, start time, completion time, validation status, quality checks, risk checks,
repair adjustments, settlement event, dispute window, and downstream revision status.

16. Proof of Time Saved as a COYN Value Vector
COYN needs more than price signals. Price can reveal demand, but it cannot fully express value, risk,
consequence, or time. The value of knowledge work depends not only on what someone pays, but on what the
work changes, what risk it reduces, what repair it avoids, and what time it restores.
Proof of Time Saved becomes one of COYN’s critical value vectors. It can help answer what work potential was
predicted, what time was saved, whose time was saved, what value was created, what risk was reduced, what
repair was avoided, what agentic compute was conserved, what human attention was restored, what state
changed, and what proof exists.
COYN measures the PoWP–PoTS loop.
Proof of Time Saved converts time sovereignty into an economic signal.

17. Use Cases
Proof of Time Saved applies wherever information, agents, and work produce accountable time compression.
In agentic research, a research Aigent activates trusted iQubes containing source material, provenance, prior
analysis, permissions, and citation constraints. PoWP predicts reduced discovery and verification time. PoTS
verifies whether the final synthesis is valid, cited, and reviewable.
In legal review, a legal Aigent reviews a contract and identifies risk, obligations, missing clauses, and
negotiation points. PoWP predicts issue spotting and review compression. PoTS applies only if legal oversight
confirms valid risk identification and reduced review time.
In healthcare navigation, a health-context iQube helps a patient or care agent identify the appropriate care
pathway faster, without exposing sensitive payloads improperly. PoTS verifies a valid care pathway, privacy
compliance, and reduced waiting or decision time.
In circular economy and sustainability, trusted material provenance can reduce time-to-audit in supply chains. A
sustainability Aigent may verify material origin, repair history, emissions claims, reuse conditions, or ecological
impact faster than fragmented manual review.
In Bitcoin proof anchoring, a high-value PoWP/PoTS receipt is hashed, aggregated, or committed into a
Bitcoin-anchored proof structure, giving the proof history a tamper-resistant timechain reference.
18. Conclusion: Speed Served Sovereignty
Proof of Time Saved exists because Time Sovereignty matters. It is not a productivity metric for extractive
systems. It is a proof mechanism for sovereign systems. It does not reward speed for its own sake. It rewards
verified acceleration toward valid outcomes.
Proof of Work Potential identifies what information may be capable of doing. Proof of Time Saved verifies what
it did. DVN records the consequence. Bitcoin can anchor proof commitments into a tamper-resistant timechain.
COYN captures the value signal.
PoWP + PoTS = Provable Time Sovereignty.
The old ethos was move fast and break things. The sovereign cybernetic ethos is move fast and fix things.
Did speed serve sovereignty?
The Knowledge Economy becomes sovereign when work potential and time saved can both be
proven.
