## 1. What Thresholds is

Thresholds is the companion article series to **The Polity Papers**.

- The Polity Papers **define** the constitutional architecture (canonical, versioned reference).
- Thresholds **reveal** — timely observational essays ("Field Notes from a Constitutional Internet")
  documenting the moment the world quietly became ready for that architecture.

Thresholds is observational, not speculative. Each article identifies a shift that has **already
occurred**, explains its constitutional implication, and introduces the civic infrastructure now
required. The intended reader reaction: *"We have already crossed a threshold. I simply hadn't
realised it yet."*

The four communication layers of the ecosystem: **The Polity Papers** (define) · **Thresholds**
(reveal) · **metaKnyts** (dramatize — mythology and culture) · **metaMe** (embody — the lived
constitutional experience). Together they explain, reveal, dramatize, and embody the Constitutional
Internet.

## 2. Editorial Canon

**The Threshold Pattern — exactly one per article.** Never introduce multiple new constitutional
ideas in one article: **one threshold · one constitutional implication · one constitutional
primitive · one emotional revelation.** (Examples of thresholds: AI Assistants → AI Representatives;
Network → Society; Platform → Constitutional Internet; Trust → Standing; Permissions → Bounded
Delegation.)

**Canonical narrative structure (six beats):**
1. **Observation** — a current event or shift; describe what happened, don't explain it yet.
2. **Hidden Change** — reveal the deeper societal shift the event exposed.
3. **The Threshold** — explicitly name the threshold crossed ("That is the threshold we've
   crossed."). The emotional/intellectual hinge.
4. **New Constitutional Reality** — why the old assumptions no longer hold; introduce at most one
   new constitutional primitive.
5. **Civic Infrastructure** — only now introduce the required infrastructure; the Polity appears as
   *one implementation* of a need already accepted, never as the destination of the argument.
6. **Invitation** — end with participation (become a citizen of the Constitutional Internet, not a
   customer of a platform).

**Voice:** essays in the register of the Financial Times, MIT Technology Review, or Wired — reveal
rather than lecture; short declarative paragraphs; one memorable idea per article; leave one
unanswered question that leads into the next Threshold.

**Editorial test:** every article should make **one idea feel inevitable** — readers should finish
thinking "Of course — that's what's been missing," not "That's an interesting proposal."

**Canonical Call to Action (the last two lines of every article, reserved and constant):**

> Claim your Polity Passport.
> Cross the threshold.

The meaning of "Cross the threshold" changes with each article; the **Passport is the constant
primitive**, the threshold is the changing context. One unified invitation, one consistent primitive,
one enduring measure of participation across the series.

## 3. Video Canon (Threshold Films)

**Hard constraint:** each film is **exactly 8.0 seconds** — begin, reveal, resolve, conclude within
8.0s; do not exceed, do not truncate the ending; hold the final CTA frame long enough to read. One
seamless cinematic sequence, one consistent visual language.

**Editorial intent:** the film does not explain the article — it creates the emotional realisation
that compels the viewer to read it ("Something fundamental has changed").

**Design language — "Precision Civic Instrumentation":** constitutional precision, calibration,
trusted guidance, human agency, constitutional bearing, quiet confidence, timeless craftsmanship.
Visual references: precision aeronautical/marine instruments, surveying equipment, Swiss
chronometers, Leica craftsmanship, Dieter Rams reduction, Leonardo da Vinci engineering studies.
**Avoid completely:** cyberpunk, neon, holograms, gaming interfaces, blockchain aesthetics, sci-fi
HUDs, technology spectacle. *Technology should feel invisible; the constitution should feel
inevitable.*

**Palette:** warm natural daylight, cream parchment, ivory, brushed gold, soft brass, warm graphite;
a gentle crimson accent **only** at the threshold moment.

**The Polity Passport (on screen):** a premium leather constitutional instrument with gold embossing
and warm ivory parchment pages. When opened, the pages remain **paper — never digital screens, never
holographic**; subtle golden "constitutional circuitry" illuminates beneath the parchment fibres, as
if the Passport is being calibrated (constitutional alignment, not electricity).

**The Constitutional Bearing Mark** (primary symbol; replaces the earlier butterfly as the primary
constitutional mark): a refined, fine-line geometric mark inspired by precision aeronautical
navigation instruments — not a compass rose, not a crypto logo, not heraldry. An elegant serif **P**
at the centre (Polity / Passport); the surrounding bearing indicator signifies constitutional
direction, trusted navigation, human agency, safe passage, constitutional orientation. It reads less
as a logo than as a constitutional instrument — "it helps you find your true bearing." (The Butterfly
Emblem persists as a *secondary* symbol for human flourishing, hope, and transformation — never
confused with the Bearing Mark.)

**The Threshold Pause (canonical cinematic device, the series' signature):** at the threshold moment
(~2–4s) time gently slows, motion almost stops, ambient particles suspend, the world appears to
inhale — then the constitutional primitive quietly appears and time resumes. The visual equivalent
of "That is the threshold we've crossed."

**Three-layer spoken cadence (only the first line changes per article):**
1. **Recognition** — the consequence, e.g. *"Societies Need Constitutions."* (the changing
   **Activation Line**)
2. **Activation** — *"Claim your Polity Passport."*
3. **Participation** — *"Cross the threshold."*

**One-sentence discipline:** every film should answer a single sentence (e.g. for "The Internet
Needs a Constitution": *"The Internet became a society. Society needs a constitution."*). Everything
else merely visualizes that realization.

## 4. ContentQube Production Pipeline

Every Threshold article ships as one canonical **ContentQube** — the complete cross-platform creative
package that enforces editorial, visual, and constitutional consistency.

**Canonical production order (the Studio Prompt and the Article are the two source-of-truth
artifacts; everything else derives from them):**

```
Canonical Article
   ↓
Creative Brief        (one-page human distillation: threshold, activation line,
   ↓                   constitutional primitive, emotional response — no cinematic detail)
Studio Prompt         (master production document — exactly 8s; camera, lighting,
   ↓                   motion, typography, Passport, mark; pasted into metaMe Studio)
Contact Sheet         (derived FROM the prompt — 4–6 frames; one selected as hero;
   ↓                   canonical rationale)
Hero Image Selection
   ↓
Hero Image Variants   (Landscape 16:9 · Portrait 4:5 · Square 1:1)
   ↓
Studio Video Prompt → Video (8s film)
   ↓
Social Package        (LinkedIn · X · Bluesky · Instagram · Threads · newsletter teaser;
   ↓                   headline · pull quote · activation quote)
CTA                   (threshold-specific activation line; always closes with
   ↓                   "Claim your Polity Passport. / Cross the threshold.")
Publish
```

Three distinct creative artefacts underlie this: **(1) Creative Brief** (one page, purely human);
**(2) Studio Prompt** (the master production document — the canonical *visual* specification, the
counterpart to the article as the canonical *editorial* specification); **(3) Contact Sheet**
(generated from the Studio Prompt — if the prompt evolves, regenerate the contact sheet, never the
reverse).

**Ontology Review** is inserted before any creative work begins: every article is checked against the
**Constitutional, Verbal, Visual, and Design ontologies** (the "Constitutional Production Test" —
nothing may contradict the constitutional canon). These four ontologies function as a shared creative
operating system: they reduce entropy and let writers, designers, and filmmakers make the same
decisions from the same constitutional worldview.

**ContentQube metadata** (makes each Threshold a reusable constitutional knowledge object): Threshold
Number · Article Title · Threshold Crossed · Constitutional Primitive · Activation Line · Hero Image ·
Video Prompt · Publication Date · Canonical URL · Related Polity Paper · Related Threshold · Passport
Primitive.

## 5. Constitutional Seeds (reusable creative DNA)

The corpus defines **Constitutional Seeds** — small, recombinable constitutional truths from which
correspondents, storytellers, filmmakers, and illustrators each begin. A KnowledgeQube may contain
many Seeds; each creator chooses one Seed and expresses it in their medium, keeping the ecosystem
coherent while allowing unlimited diversity. The three "evergreen" Seeds singled out as generative:
**The Threshold**, **Privacy with Accountability**, and **Action Gives Standing**. (The full set of
ten Seeds is the source for the invariants staged in this pass; see the canonization report.)

## 6. Canonical Ontology

The ontology separates institution, credential, experience, and representative into distinct roles
(seeded as `inv.polity.249`/`.250`):

```
Constitutional Internet
│
├── Human Agency Field
│     (experiential medium)
│
├── The Polity
│     (civic institution)
│
├── Polity Passport
│     (constitutional credential)
│
├── metaMe
│     (constitutional experience layer)
│
└── aigentMe
      (bounded constitutional representative)
```

- **The Polity** — the civic institution; one implementation of the constitutional infrastructure the
  Internet now requires, not the destination of the argument.
- **The Polity Passport** — the constitutional credential; answers *"what are you authorised to do?"*.
- **metaMe** — the constitutional experience layer; the environment through which a citizen
  participates. People do not *use* metaMe; they participate *through* it.
- **aigentMe** — the citizen's bounded constitutional representative, one of the primary participants
  within metaMe.
- **Human Agency Field** — the experiential medium in which the whole ontology is situated (the
  subject of a forthcoming paper).
