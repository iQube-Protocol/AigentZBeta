# Standing Framework

**Version 1.0.1 · Ratified 2026-06-17 · Binds Constitution 1.0.0 · Operational companion to the Standing Charter**

This is the operational companion to **The Standing Charter** (the canonical
constitutional definition). The Charter defines what Standing *is* — confidence
in the veracity of declarations. This Framework describes how it operates today.

## Who holds Standing

Standing is held by **all participating entities** — citizens, aigents,
organizations, and institutions. Standing is **not** citizenship.

- **Citizens** (human) hold Standing, citizenship, and inalienable rights.
- **Participant agents** (participant-passport-bearing) **do hold Standing**.
  They do **not** hold citizenship or inalienable rights — their rights are
  **participatory and revocable**, and they hold no governance authority.

Standing measures veracity confidence; it never confers citizenship, governance
authority, or inalienable rights.

## Standing dimensions (operational)

Standing is event-driven, never time-driven. The operational store
(`crm_persona_reputation`) tracks three lanes:

- **Personal** — confidence from an entity's own verified declarations and
  contributions.
- **Delegated** — confidence accruing to a sponsor from the contributions of the
  participants/agents they sponsor.
- **Stewardship** — how effectively an entity cultivates those it sponsors.

## Relationship to the metaCommons

Standing signals are one of the Commons Signal sources (see **The metaCommons
Charter**). Standing contributes to **Proof of Work Potential**; realized value
is verified through **Proof of Time Saved**. PoWP and PoTS are defined in the
Polity Paper series (Qriptopian codex), pending ingestion as constitutional
commentary.
