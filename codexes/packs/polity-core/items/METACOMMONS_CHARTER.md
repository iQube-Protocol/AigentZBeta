# The metaCommons Charter

**Version 1.0.0 · Ratified 2026-06-17 · Status: ratified · Polity Core constitutional document**

## Purpose

The metaCommons is the second institution of the Polity.

Its purpose is to transform sovereign signals into collective intelligence while
preserving privacy, sovereignty, and citizen rights.

## The Commons

- The Commons is not a database.
- The Commons is not a marketplace.
- The Commons is not a social network.

The Commons is a **field** — a continuously evolving field generated from the
interaction of sovereign assets and sovereign activity across the Polity.

## Sources of Commons Signal

Commons Signals emerge from:

- metaQube metadata
- DVN receipts
- Intent declarations
- Capability declarations
- Standing signals
- Experience outcomes
- Opportunity signals

## The Commons and Proof of Work Potential

The metaCommons aggregates Proof of Work Potential across the Polity. Its role is
to surface:

- unmet demand,
- latent capability,
- emerging opportunity,
- coordination opportunities,
- venture opportunities.

## Commons Learning

The Commons continuously learns through Proof of Time Saved.

Its purpose is not to judge citizens. Its purpose is to improve the Polity's
ability to align truthful information with valuable outcomes.

## Constitutional Principle

The metaCommons exists to discover capability latent within the Polity and make
that capability legible without compromising sovereignty.
