# Invariant Intelligence

**Foundational Constitutional Record · Version 0.1 · Status: Canonical Foundational Record · Date: July 2026**

- **Project:** Chrysalis Foundation
- **Institution:** The Polity

## Purpose

This document establishes the constitutional record for the discovery and
formulation of the computational principles collectively referred to as
**Invariant Intelligence**.

It serves as the canonical reference for the architectural evolution of the
Chrysalis platform and the constitutional foundation upon which future
specifications, implementations, and research relating to Invariant Intelligence
are based.

Its purpose is not merely to describe an implementation, but to record the
first-principle discoveries that underpin a new model for intelligence systems
founded upon invariants, compressed reasoning, and adaptive composition.

## Foundational Observation

Human civilization advances not through the accumulation of information, but
through the discovery, preservation, and propagation of invariants.

- Reasoning transforms information into invariants.
- Those invariants constitute reusable knowledge.
- Knowledge enables capability.
- Capability produces consequence.

This progression forms the computational foundation of Invariant Intelligence.

## Constitutional Principles

The following principles are recognized as foundational.

**Principle I** — Reasoning discovers invariants.

**Principle II** — Invariants constitute knowledge.

**Principle III** — Knowledge precedes inference.

**Principle IV** — Compressed expertise is a reusable computational primitive.

**Principle V** — Adaptive systems should compose validated primitives rather
than repeatedly rediscover them.

**Principle VI** — Architectural evolution preserves validated invariants while
extending their composition.

**Principle VII** — Civilizational progress is the progressive accumulation of
validated invariants.

## Architectural Philosophy

Invariant Intelligence adopts the following engineering philosophy.

- Preserve before Replace
- Extend before Recreate
- Compose before Specialize
- Discover Invariants before Designing Abstractions
- Compress Expertise before Automating It
- Separate Reasoning from Inference
- Separate Architecture from Rendering
- Treat Invariants as First-Class Computational Primitives

## Constitutional Intent

Invariant Intelligence is intended as an extension of existing computational
systems rather than a replacement for them.

Its objective is to provide the semantic and constitutional substrate through
which existing models, infrastructures, protocols, and intelligent systems may
operate with greater coherence, explainability, composability, and capability.
