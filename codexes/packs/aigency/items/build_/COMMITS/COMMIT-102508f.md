Ratify Invariant Fields ontology (CFS-002 §2a) + generalize Law XV

Law XV legislated field BEHAVIOUR (composition is multiplicative,
fields are locally independent/globally dependent) without the
ontology ever defining what a field IS. CFS-002 gains that missing
layer, co-authored operator + agent:

- Universal definition: an Invariant Field is a coherent domain of
  interacting invariants governing a system's behaviour — deliberately
  system-agnostic (no human, AI, experience, or software named), on
  the observation that the same compositional mathematics governs
  biology (DNA defines fields; the organism is the composition),
  engineering (a bridge is the emergent consequence of material/
  geometric/stress/environmental/construction fields), operating
  systems (Linux is the composition, not the fields), civilization,
  and intelligence — not only constitutional experience.
- Field vs. Collection vs. Namespace: three axes, not one hierarchy.
  A field is a functional ROLE occupied during composition, not a
  container — keeps storage (Collection), classification (Namespace),
  and execution (Field) unconflated. Explains why the Semantic Field
  spans three namespaces (constitutional/reasoning/capability) while
  Narrative/Style happen to share their namespace's name by
  convenience, not requirement.
- Natural fields (discovered — gravitation, genetics, thermodynamics)
  vs. constitutional fields (ratified — governance, protocols,
  experience architectures): same mathematics, different origin.
- The universal hierarchy (Invariant → Collection → Field → System →
  Observable Behaviour → Consequences) with Experience demoted to one
  observable behaviour among many, not the hierarchy's terminus; the
  Constitutional Internet's flywheel (CFS-006a) is this hierarchy's
  constitutional specialization.
- The five constitutional fields (Semantic/Narrative/Style/
  Experience/Context), each answering a distinct question no other
  field answers, plus Corollaries III (Experience Emergence — composed,
  not stored) and IV (Constitutional Explainability — decomposable back
  to source fields).
