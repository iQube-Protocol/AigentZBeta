### Option 1: The Agentic Launchpad (Trust-as-a-Service)

[cite_start]Instead of just launching tokens, we build a platform that **proves** a project is safe before anyone invests[cite: 153, 195].

* [cite_start]**The Concept**: New projects mint a "Project iQube" containing their smart contract audits and team identities[cite: 155].
* [cite_start]**The Tech**: We use the **Proof of Risk** framework to have our agents automatically scan these iQubes[cite: 174, 210].
* [cite_start]**Engineering Goal**: We will implement the logic that translates a raw audit (stored in a blakQube) into a public "Trust Score" on-chain[cite: 173, 205].
* [cite_start]**Why it’s cool**: It turns our registry into a security standard for the entire crypto ecosystem[cite: 156, 168].

---

### Option 2: The Ghost Concierge (Privacy-First Assistant)

[cite_start]We turn the iQube into a high-tech "Vault" that manages your digital life without leaking your secrets[cite: 152, 158].

* [cite_start]**The Concept**: Users store sensitive info like passwords, API keys, and IDs in blakQubes[cite: 155]. 
* [cite_start]**The Tech**: When another app needs your info, our agent acts as a "Ghost Concierge"[cite: 162]. [cite_start]It uses **Capability Tokens** and **A2A (Agent-to-Agent)** messaging to provide "proof" of the data without ever showing the actual password[cite: 182, 205].
* [cite_start]**Engineering Goal**: We need to build an **MCP (Model Context Protocol) server** so the agent can safely "call" these credentials to perform tasks[cite: 182, 231].
* **Why it’s cool**: This is the "Victory Lap" for our stack. [cite_start]It uses almost every advanced feature in the 2026 brief, from **Selective Disclosure** to **Tool Interop**[cite: 148, 190].
